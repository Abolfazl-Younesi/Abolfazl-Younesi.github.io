About

Abolfazl Younesi (Student Member, IEEE) is pursuing a PhD at the University of Innsbruck, Austria, where he serves as a University Assistant in the Department of Computer Science. He is a member of the Distributed and Parallel Systems Group (DPS) and actively contributes to European Union research projects focused on advancing distributed computing technologies. His doctoral research investigates critical challenges in modern distributed systems, with particular emphasis on intelligent resource management, time-sensitive scheduling algorithms, and compute optimization strategies across the computing continuum. His work spans multiple domains including stream processing architectures, federated learning systems, and edge-to-cloud computing paradigms. Through his research, he aims to develop novel approaches that enhance the efficiency, reliability, and scalability of distributed computing infrastructures, particularly in resource-constrained and heterogeneous environments. His contributions address both theoretical foundations and practical implementations, bridging the gap between academic research and real-world deployment scenarios in distributed systems.
